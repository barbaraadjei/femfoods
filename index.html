Buvette

Chef Jody Williams’ Buvette opened its doors in early 2011. The gastrothèque is known in the West Village as “a tiny slice of Paris. The French bistro’s rustic, home decor, coupled with its eggs scrambled to perfection, makes it a perfect place to grab brunch on a Sunday morning. In 2018, Williams was named as a finalist for the prestigious James Beard Award title for best New York City chef.

(212) 255-3590 | learn more
42 Grove St, New York, NY 10014

Good Enough To Eat

After studying at Pru Leith’s School of Food and Wine in London, Carrie Levin moved to New Yok City in 1979. Two years later, Levin opened Good Enough To Eat. The French chef cited a desire to serve “good, old-fashioned American food.” Their all day breakfast menu featuring omelets, pancakes, waffles, and buttermilk biscuits with strawberry butter.

(212) 496-0163 | learn more
520 Columbus Ave, New York, NY 10024

Prune

Gabrielle Hamilton is the chef and owner of Prune, which she opened in October 1999. People go to Prune because the food remind them about how delicious and ambitious, and there’s a no-nonsense vibe that reflects Hamilton’s sense of confidence. Hamilton is also author of New York Times bestseller Blood, Bones, and Butter. In 2018, Hamilton won the NYC James Beard Award for outstanding chef due to Prune's continued excellence.

(212) 677-6221 | learn more
54 East 1st Street, New York, NY 10003

MacDonough Café

MacDonough Café was opened by Nadia Busseuil and Benoit Busseuil in 2014 in Bedford-Stuyvesant, Brooklyn. They focused on fresh and healthy food, something they felt was missing from the neighborhood. Ninety percent of all the café's products are organic, and all of their food is made from scratch. The coffee shop is family-friendly and includes coloring books and a kids' menu.

(347) 221-0295 | learn more
83 Saratoga Ave, Brooklyn, NY 11233

Hudson Jane

Partners Jen LaPorta and Megan Johnson open Hudson Jane was opened on Fort Greene at 360 Myrtle Avenue in 2017. When they open the cafe many people in the neighbors stop by get breakfast, lunch and dinner, wine and/or other beverages, and desserts. Hudson Jane's starts the day with breakfast sandwiches, burritos, and pastries before moving on to salads and sandwiches for lunch, and ending the day with an eclectic menu with ceviche, a patty melt, and chicken liver paté.

(347) 987-3881 | learn more
360 Myrtle Ave, Brooklyn, NY 11205

Vinatería

New York native Yvette Leeper-Bueno began as a fashion entrepreneur and carried that spirit to the culinary world, opening Vinatería in 2013. The Harlem restaurant and winebar is best known for its Spanish and Italian inspired dishes, as well as its fresh, contemporary décor. Vinatería has received much acclaim, receiving attention from the Financial Times as one of the five best Italian restaurants in the city.

(212) 662-8462 | learn more
2211 Frederick Douglass Blvd, New York, NY 10026

I Sodi

Chef and owner Rita Sodi brought her culinary passion and talents from a small farm north of Florence to one of the most acclaimed restaurants in Manhattan. Her travels (and her mother’s Tuscan cooking) inspired her to share her passion for food with others. I Sodi’s menu is steeped in tradition and simplicity, and Sodi still imports her oil from her native Italy.

(212) 414-5774 | learn more
105 Christopher Street New York, NY 10014

Via Carota

Three months after the launch of I Sodi, Rita Sodi met Jody Williams. The two co-chefs/owners have balanced their personal and professional lives to form one of the most sought-after restaurants in the West Village. Sodi and Williams established this Italian gastroteca in 2014. Via Carota combines Sodi’s Tuscan roots and Williams’ experience cooking in Emilia-Romagna and Rome to create a menu loyal to Italian tradition.

(212) 255-1962 | learn more
51 Grove St, New York, NY 10014

The Sosta

Inspired by gas stations seen along the highways of Italy, Executive Chef Ali LaRaia and Creative Director Samantha Wasser co-founded The Sosta in 2016. The fast-casual restaurant draws its namesake from the Italian word for ‘break’. The Sosta is a fantastic retreat for any diner looking for the simplicity and authenticity of an fresh Italian meal, whether its Gemelli pesto or the fresh-baked focaccia.

(212) 988-9028 | learn more
186 Mott Street, New York, NY 10012

King

Fittingly named after its location on King Street, this Soho establishment specializes in Southern Italian cuisine. Chefs Jess Shadbolt and Clare de Boer and general manager Annie Shi focus on simplicity, from the locally-sourced produce to the light, reflective windows and mirrors. Among King’s more popular dishes are the wild halibut grilled with asparagus and the pink chicory salad.

(917) 825-1618 | learn more
18 King Street, New York, NY 10014

Bessou

Bessou roughly translates to “home away from home,” and that’s exactly what Maiko Kyogoku intended the NoHo fusion restaurant to be. Bessou is a modern take on classic Japanese comfort food. Popular items on its seasonal menu include eggplant shakshuka with miso tofu labne, wagyu steak and eggs, and dan dan udon noodles. Kyogoku’s personal favorite? The damako rice balls she fondly remembers cooking with her mother and sister.

(212) 228-8502 | learn more
5 Bleecker Street, New York, NY 10012

Baoburg

Chef Suchanan Aksornnan, also lovingly known as Chef Bao Bao, is originally from Thailand. The fresh produce and flavors she grew up around sparked her early interest in food. Chef Bao Bao got her start at The French Culinary Institute in NYC and fuses her French training and Thai roots at this Greenpoint pan-Asian restaurant. In 2015, Chef Bao Bao was named byZagat as one of their ‘30 under 30’ rising culinary stars in the city.

(718) 349–0011 | learn more
614 Manhattan Ave, Brooklyn, NY 11222

mŏkbar

Chef-owner Esther Chao grew up in New Jersey but always felt a deep connection to her Korean heritage. Preparing traditional dishes with her grandmother allowed Chao to explore her passion for Korean cuisine, which she know shares at this Chelsea Market noodle bar. mŏkbar opened its doors in 2014 and has received numerous accolades since: the restaurant was Village Voice’s 2014 Reader’s Choice “Best New Restaurant” and was awarded “Best Kimchi” by Epoch Taste in 2015.

(646) 775-1169 | learn more
75 9th Avenue New York, NY 10011

Café China

Café China, the lone Michelin star Chinese restaurant in New York City, was opened by Yiming Wang and her husband Xian Zhang in 2011. This Midtown establishment is a masterclass in Sichuan cuisine. Recommended dishes from New York Times food critic Pete Wells include the spicy diced rabbit, tea-smoked duck, baby ribs wrapped in bamboo leaves, and chungking spicy chicken.

(212) 213-2810 | learn more
13 East 37th Street New York, NY 10016

Sylvia's

Sylvia’s restaurant was found by the late Sylvia Woods in 1962. Sylvia is a Harlem favourite, and she's been serving soul food for over 55 years. Many people called the restaurant World Restaurant or Kitchen. The restaurant also sells prepared foods, beauty and skin care items, cookbooks, and a children's book written by Woods. Sylvia's is well-frequented by high-status celebrities and civil rights officials alike.

(212) 996-0660 | learn more
328 Malcolm X Blvd, New York, NY 10027

Cheryl's Global Soul

When Cheryl Smith was opening a restaurant, she knew she was taking a big risk. Cheryl's Global Soul first opened up in Brooklyn in 2006 and has remained a neighborhood staple ever since. Cheryl wanted a place where locals could come in several times a day, get their coffee in the morning, and come then back for dessert at night.

(347) 529-2855 | learn more
236 Underhill Ave, Brooklyn, NY 1123

Melba's

Melba Wilson is one of the most successful African-American women in the restaurant and catering business. In 2004, she decided to open her own restaurant. Today, she is the proud sole owner of three Harlem-based businesses: Melba’s Restaurant, Melba’s Catering and a 350-seat dining hall and cafeteria on 125th street called Melba’s 125. In 2015, Melba celebrated 10 years in business, serving more than 2 million guests via the restaurant & catering.

(212) 864-7777 | learn more
300 W 114th St, New York, NY 10026

DŌ

Kristen Tomlan turned her affinity for baking and desserts into a business, opening a store dedicated to cookie dough in early 2017. Cookie DŌ NYC became an immediate sensation and has been featured in Buzzfeed, Time, and Forbes, just to name a few. This small shop in Greenwich Village boasts a batch of unique flavours, ranging from Fluffernutter to Brookie Dough. Lines are quite long, but everyone agrees that the cookie dough is well worth the wait.

(646) 892-3600 | learn more
550 LaGuardia Pl, New York, NY 10012

Ovenly Greenpoint

Erin Patinkin and Agatha Kulaga connected at a food-focused bookclub, and the rest was history. Ovenly began at this Greenpoint location in 2010. The pair draw heavily from their Eastern European roots and their love of sweet-savory pastries. Patikin and Kulaga also put a premium on social justice, partnering with Getting Out Staying Out and the Ansob Center for Refugees to train a diverse group of employees who also share their love for baked goods.

(888) 899-2213 | learn more
31 Greenpoint Ave, Brooklyn NY 11222

Clementine Bakery

Michelle Barton got her start in the fashion world but never abandoned her love for baking. She opened Clementine Bakery in 2012 with the goal of catering to vegans who happen to have a sweet tooth as well. All of her cakes, pies, and pastries are homemade and 100% vegan. But make no mistake—this Bed-Stuy bakery is popular among vegans and omnivores alike.

(718) 483-8788 | learn more
299 Greene Ave, Brooklyn, NY 11238

Milk Bar Williamsburg

Milk Bar has several locations across Manhattan and Brooklyn, but the idea was born at this Williamsburg shop in 2008. Chef and owner Christina Tosi was working at Momofuku when her unconventional homemade cookies caught the eye of her boss, eventually prompting her to start her own business. When she's not judging on Top Chef, Tosi is constantly perfecting her cookie recipes, and even has what she lovingly calls “The Broken Cookie Bin.”

(347) 577-9504 | learn more
382 Metropolitan Ave, Brooklyn, NY 11211
